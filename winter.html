Winter Activities

Summary of winter activities

Winter is magically romantic with a crackling wood fire, a glass of champagne and your own bubbling Whirlpool Bath Tub and private rejuvenating Hot Tub, in-room real crackling wood fireplace and with romantic 1st Class gourmet dining.

Picture perfect with pure white snow covered trees and lakes sparkling like diamonds in the snow. Enjoy a surreal evening of walking under the most brilliant stars. For those who need a little help getting outdoors we even have all the outdoor clothing for you if you decide to participate.

Wildness Nature

Feed Canada Jay & Chickadee

Feed Canada's new official birds Canada Jay (aka the Gray Jay or the Whisky Jack) and Boreal Chickadee from your hand or your hat.

For the Aficionado Birder check the latest winter birding report for what is being seen and where:

  1. Algonquin Logging Museum Trail
  2. Spruce Bog Boardwalk Trail
  3. Mew Lake Old Railway Trail

Snow Adventures

Cross Country Skiing & Snowshoeing

Tranquil with peacefulness. Algonquin Park has International quality ski trails;
track set and skating lane.

48 kilometers of groomed and 23 kilometers of non-groomed cross-country ski trails available in three low-usage trail networks, typically December to early April. Check the latest ski trail conditions. If you cannot go skiing you can always go IceHiking.

Provided Free to use:

  • Wax-less cross-country skis, boots and poles
  • Waxing station for those that enjoy waxing
  • Pro snowshoes

Serene spots for Snowshoeing:

  1. Algonquin outdoor Logging Museum Trail
  2. Spruce Bog Boardwalk Trail
  3. Mew Lake Old Railway Trail

Algonquin Park

Algonquin Visitor Center

Snow-covered Algonquin Park boasts pristine beauty and world-class venues, and we proudly provide a Free Park Pass for your adventure.

Algonquin Park's Visitor Center is open daily, has exhibits on the Park's natural and human history, a relaxing restaurant, an excellent bookstore, theatre and a viewing deck of the Winter wild Algonquin landscape.
